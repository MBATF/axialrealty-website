Collin County's Next Frontier — $1.5B in New Development

Neighborhood Guides › Anna, TX

Overview

Anna is where North Texas's next chapter is being written. The city recently made national headlines as one of the top rapidly expanding communities in the country — its population grew approximately 177% between 2022 and 2023 alone. In March 2026, Sherley Farms broke ground on a $1.5 billion master-planned community bringing roughly 3,000 homes to the city, signaling that institutional investment has arrived in force.

Average home prices around $359,000 make Anna one of the most affordable new-construction options in all of Collin County — significantly below Frisco, Prosper, and even Celina. The Collin County Outer Loop infrastructure investment is also improving Anna's connectivity to the broader DFW corridor, opening up commuting options that didn't exist a few years ago.

The Vibe

Anna still feels like a small town — because it is one. Main Street has that genuine community feel, local Friday night football games draw the whole city, and neighbors actually know each other. But that small-town character is being tested by rapid growth, and how Anna manages that transition will define the next decade.

For early movers, the appeal is clear: lower prices, newer homes, and the potential for meaningful appreciation as development fills in around you. Job growth in nearby Sherman — where Texas Instruments and Global Wafers have semiconductor plans underway — is creating additional employment gravity in Anna's direction.

Lifestyle Highlights

  • Sherley Farms — The $1.5B master-planned community that broke ground in March 2026; 3,000+ homes coming with full amenity packages
  • Anna ISD — A growing district with strong community support; expanding to accommodate rapid population growth
  • Collin County Outer Loop — Major infrastructure investment connecting Anna to McKinney, Frisco, and the broader metro
  • Sherman employment corridor — Texas Instruments and Global Wafers semiconductor facilities nearby create significant job gravity
  • Affordability — Average home prices around $359K; among the lowest in Collin County for new construction
  • Small-town authenticity — Friday night lights, local community events, and a genuine Main Street feel that larger suburbs have lost

Who It's For

Anna is for buyers who want the best price-per-square-foot in Collin County, early movers who want to capture appreciation ahead of full build-out, families who value small-town community culture, and remote workers or those with flexible commutes who can trade location for space and value.
