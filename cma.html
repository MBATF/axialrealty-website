Received a higher-than-expected appraisal notice from your county? Request a free Comparative Market Analysis and get the data you need to protest effectively.

How It Works

We Pull the Comps.
You File the Protest.

Your county appraisal district sets your home's taxable value — and they don't always get it right. A CMA gives you real sales data from comparable homes in your neighborhood to challenge an inflated appraisal and potentially lower your property tax bill.

1

Submit Your Request

Fill out the form with your property address and the appraisal value shown on your notice. Takes less than 2 minutes.

2

David Pulls the Comps

David Tung will run a Comparative Market Analysis using recent sales data from the MLS to determine what your home is actually worth in today's market.

3

You Receive Your CMA

We'll send your free CMA report by email. Use it as supporting evidence when you file your protest with your county appraisal district.

4

File Your Protest

Submit your protest online or by mail before your county's deadline (typically May 15 or 30 days after receiving your notice, whichever is later).

Which counties do we cover?

We pull comps for properties in Collin County, Denton County, Dallas County, Tarrant County, and Rockwall County — covering Frisco, Plano, Allen, McKinney, Prosper, Celina, Melissa, Anna, Lewisville, Richardson, Coppell, Flower Mound, and surrounding DFW communities.
