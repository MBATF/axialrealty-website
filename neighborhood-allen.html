Living in Allen, TX
Top-Ranked Schools, Boutique Shopping & a True Community Feel

Neighborhood Guides › Allen, TX

Overview

Allen punches well above its size. Squeezed between McKinney and Plano along the SH-121 corridor, Allen has consistently ranked among the safest and most livable cities in the United States — and residents feel it. Allen ISD is one of the most respected school districts in Texas, Allen Eagle football is a genuine cultural institution, and the city's retail and dining scene is remarkably strong for its population.

Recent development is accelerating Allen's profile. The Farm at Allen along SH-121 is becoming a major lifestyle destination, with Wood Partners' 311-unit Alta Preserve luxury apartments anchoring the district. The city's $17M Downtown Catalyst Project is also revitalizing historic downtown Allen with new restaurants, parks, and public spaces.

The Vibe

Allen is the suburb that feels like a real community. People come for the schools and stay because they genuinely like it here. Star Creek is one of the most sought-after neighborhoods in the entire metroplex — gated, elegant, and deeply established. Twin Creeks and Watters Creek give residents resort-style amenity packages without the Frisco price tag.

Weekends in Allen revolve around the Allen Event Center (minor league hockey and soccer), the Allen Premium Outlets, and Watters Creek's outdoor shopping and dining village.

Lifestyle Highlights

  • Allen ISD — Consistently A-rated; Allen Eagle football is legendary in Texas high school athletics
  • Allen Event Center — Home to the Allen Americans hockey team and Dallas Sidekicks soccer; a genuine anchor for weekend entertainment
  • Watters Creek at Montgomery Farm — Outdoor shopping and dining village with a creek walk, boutiques, and restaurants in a walkable setting
  • Allen Premium Outlets — High-end outlet shopping, easily accessible from most Allen neighborhoods
  • Don Rodenbaugh Natatorium — 35,000+ sq ft indoor aquatic facility; one of the best community swim facilities in the metro
  • The Edge Skate Park — 35,000 sq ft outdoor skate park drawing riders from across DFW
  • The Farm at Allen — Rapidly developing lifestyle district along SH-121 with luxury apartments, dining, and retail coming online

Who It's For

Allen is the sweet spot for families who want elite schools and community without paying Frisco prices. It draws young families, Allen ISD loyalists, and professionals working along the 121 or 75 corridors. Star Creek appeals to luxury buyers who want gated privacy; Twin Creeks suits resort-lifestyle seekers; and Ridgeview Crossing offers strong value for growing families.
